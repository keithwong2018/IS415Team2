2. Mapping the spatial disparities in urban healthcare services using taxi trajectories data

Study Objectives:
To analyse the demand and supply of health care services and the spatial accessibility of healthcare services using the taxi trajectories data

Methodology:

  1. Gravity model to evaluate the relative ease of reaching the spatially distributed resource/services
    • Gravity Model uses the theory that the attraction between two objects is proportional to their mass and inversely proportional to their respective distance
  2. 2-Step Floating Catchment Area Method (2SFCA)
    • Special case of gravity model developed to measure spatial accessibility
    • Supply: potential relative amount of population in community i served by hospital j
    • Demand: calculation of accessibility, considering the effects of population, hospital capacity and spatial impedance (distance)
  3. Standardization of demand & supply using Z-score method to illustrate the status of healthcare services
  4. Learning Points:
    • 2-SFCA derives an Accessibility Index to determine the accessibility of services to communities, which is applicable to our study
  5. Areas for Improvement:
    • The analysis uses only taxi networks to determine the demand & accessibility of hospitals, which underrepresents the actual number of hospital visits as visitors may arrive by other modes of transport
    • Taxi networks are unable to differentiate the specific purposes of hospital visits; each hospital arrival may not accurately represent the demand
    • The taxi network data is taken from only one week, which does not fully represent all hospital arrivals in a year
